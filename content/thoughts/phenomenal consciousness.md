*a 650nm patch gives the lab a wavelength, the retina a cone response, and the subject a red look.*

- phenomenal consciousness points at the felt quality: the redness of red, the painfulness of pain
- it is what [[thoughts/access consciousness]] can route around without explaining
- nagel (1974) defines it as "what it is like" to be in a state
- kriegel splits this into qualitative (what appears) and subjective (that it appears for someone)

### access vs phenomenal
- block (1995) decouples the two
- **[[thoughts/access consciousness]]**: information available for reasoning and report
- phenomenal experience overflows access
- blindsight suggests information guides action without experience

### the hard problem
- chalmers (1995) divides easy problems (functional explanation) from the hard problem
- the hard problem asks why processing feels like anything
- levine (1983) terms this the explanatory gap: physical processes fail to explain phenomenal quality

### thought experiments
- **mary's room**: jackson (1982) argues a color scientist in a black-and-white room learns something new upon seeing red
- **what is it like to be a bat**: nagel (1974) states subjective character ties to a single point of view inaccessible from outside
- **[[thoughts/philosophical zombies]]**: chalmers (1996) conceives functional duplicates lacking experience, concluding consciousness evades physical explanation
- **inverted spectrum**: shoemaker (1982) imagines identical function with inverted qualia, decoupling qualitative content from functional role

### theories
- **higher-order theories**: consciousness requires a higher-order thought targeting a mental state
- **global workspace theory**: specialized processors compete for access; explains access consciousness while leaving phenomenality untouched
- **integrated information theory**: consciousness requires integrated information (Φ); implies panpsychism and faces computational intractability
- **representationalism**: phenomenal character reduces to representational content; threatened by inverted spectrum cases

### AI systems
- other minds problem amplifies due to different substrates and lack of shared lineage
- behavior gives access evidence: reports, task changes, internal circuits
- the phenomenal variable remains unobserved

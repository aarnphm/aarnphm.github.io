title: Euler's identity

$$
\begin{aligned}
e^{i \pi} &+ 1 = 0 \\
\\
\because e &: \text{Euler's number} \\
i &: \text{imaginary unit satisfies } i^{2} = -1 \\
\pi &: \text{pi}
\end{aligned}
$$

Special case of Euler's formula:

$$
e^{i \theta} = \cos(\theta) + i \sin(\theta)
$$

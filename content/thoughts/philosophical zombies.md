_when i reach for p-zombies, i apply a wrench to the habit of treating "same function" as "same mind."_

- a p-zombie keeps the causal graph fixed (input-output links, memory updates, behavioral reports)
- tests whether anything had to be present on the inside
- poses a hinge question about [[thoughts/identity]]: consciousness either reduces to physical facts or sits beside them

### chalmers's compression

- **conceivability**: imagining a physically identical world where creatures lack phenomenal consciousness involves no contradiction
- **possibility**: if conceivable, they are metaphysically possible
- **anti-materialism**: if possible, physical facts fail to necessitate phenomenal facts

### conceivability debate

- the true zombie matches every functional detail and passes every behavioral test while lacking [[thoughts/qualia]]
- dennett denies this stability, arguing fully specified functional organization inherently includes consciousness
- the zombie intuition counters that control-flow charts lack nodes for "redness"

### the gap

- water-without-h₂o remains conceivable but impossible (conceptual gap avoids metaphysical gap)
- chalmers claims consciousness differs because the gap avoids closing a posteriori
- three exits:
  1. **deny conceivability**: claim functional duplicates include consciousness and owe an account of why
  2. **deny the inference**: argue phenomenal and physical concepts interact weirdly, misleading imagination
  3. **accept possibility, downgrade drama**: consciousness ties to physics nomologically without metaphysical necessity

### nearby probes

- **inverted spectrum**: same function, different phenomenology ([[thoughts/inverted spectrum]])
- **china brain**: implemented organization falls short for experience
- **absent qualia**: partial function without phenomenology

### modern relevance

- **ai consciousness**: functional tests leave the question of internal experience open
- **mechanistic interpretability**: causal maps remain silent about experience
- **clinical criteria**: if consciousness and function decouple, clinical instruments only approximate what we care about
- use the zombie case to find where a theory pays: deny the duplicate, break the conceivability-to-possibility move, or give up strict physical necessity

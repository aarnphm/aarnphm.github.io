whenever i think about inverted spectrum cases, i'm stress-testing how tightly i want to tie phenomenology to function.

the hook: you and i both stop at red lights, sort tomatoes from grass, and pass every color test. the swap sits inside: your tomato-look is my grass-look.

if that picture is coherent, functional role does not fix phenomenal character. same function, different [[thoughts/qualia|consciousness]]. Locke has an early version of the thought. Shoemaker gives the modern pressure-test form. [@locke1689essay; @shoemaker1982invertedspectrum]

## the setup

1. **functional equivalence.** same discriminations, reports, memories, associations, and behavior.
2. **phenomenal inversion.** different color experience under the same functional role.

if both can coexist, bare [[thoughts/functionalism]] loses a clean identity claim. "red-experience" cannot mean only the causal role, because the same causal role can carry a different feel.

the argument is weaker than [[thoughts/philosophical zombies|zombies]] because it keeps experience in the world. it is also harder to dismiss. absence sounds metaphysically wild. swapping red and green sounds annoyingly stable.

## where the setup creaks

color space has structure. unique hues, opponent channels, brightness, saturation, memory, affect, and use all hang together. a clean red/green inversion has to preserve enough of that structure to remain behaviorally invisible. Hardin pushes on this point: color phenomenology may carry internal relations that cannot be permuted freely. [@hardin1988color]

learned associations add another snag. blood, stop signs, fire trucks, warning lights. if your red-quale is my green-quale, does that green-feel carry danger for you? Shoemaker's move is developmental: if inversion is congenital, the associations form around the inverted feel from the start. the public role stays fixed. [@shoemaker1982invertedspectrum]

the neural reply says human color architecture already binds the functional and phenomenal structures. maybe cones, opponent processes, and cortical color areas leave no room for inversion. that may be true about humans. the philosophical case asks whether function alone explains why.

## functionalist exits

Dennett denies that the case is genuinely conceivable. once the functional story is fully specified, there is no leftover quale to invert. [@dennett1988quining]

another route expands the role: include phenomenal character inside the functional specification. [@levin2024functionalism] that blocks inversion by definition. it also feels like smuggling the thing to be explained into the explanation.

representationalism says phenomenal character is representational content: red-experience represents red-making surface properties. if the representation is the same, the feel is the same. [@tye1995ten] the inverted-spectrum reply is that same represented content might still arrive in a different format.

## why it still matters

for AI, the case says behavior and causal maps may underspecify experience. two systems could pass the same tests and expose the same [[thoughts/mechanistic interpretability|mechanistic]] circuits while differing phenomenally, if qualia are not fixed by function.

for other minds, it is the ordinary uncertainty with the volume turned up. i can know how you sort tomatoes. i cannot step into the exact color of your tomato.

for [[thoughts/knowledge argument|Mary]], it gives the sibling intuition: complete functional knowledge can leave out what the state is like.

## Wittgenstein's knife

[[thoughts/Wittgenstein|Wittgenstein]] asks whether a private inversion has content if no public criterion can ever mark the difference. If every use of "red" stays fixed, what makes it true that the private item differs? [@wittgenstein1953pi]

that does not dissolve the itch for me. it does locate the soft spot. the inverted spectrum depends on there being a fact about private phenomenal sameness that outruns every public test.

the useful output is modest: function gives the public color map. the case asks whether the private color-map can vary while that public map stays fixed.

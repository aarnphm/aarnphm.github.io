## about toronto.

Toronto is Canada's largest city and a vibrant cultural hub. From the iconic CN Tower to diverse neighborhoods, world-class museums, and a thriving food scene, there's always something new to explore.

The city sits on the traditional territory of many nations including the Mississaugas of the Credit, the Anishnabeg, the Chippewa, the Haudenosaunee and the Wendat peoples.

## must-visit places.

### kensington market

A bohemian neighborhood filled with vintage shops, cafes, and diverse food vendors. Perfect for a leisurely afternoon stroll.

### distillery district

Historic cobblestone streets lined with Victorian-era buildings now housing art galleries, restaurants, and boutiques.

### high park

Toronto's largest public park featuring hiking trails, a zoo, cherry blossoms in spring, and beautiful lakeside views.

### royal ontario museum

One of North America's largest museums, showcasing art, culture, and natural history from around the world.

## food & coffee.

Toronto's food scene is incredibly diverse. Here are a few favorites:

- **dinette triple crown** - cozy brunch spot in little portugal
- **pai** - exceptional thai food in the entertainment district
- **st. lawrence market** - historic market with amazing peameal bacon sandwiches
- **uncle tetsu's** - japanese cheesecake that melts in your mouth
- **balzac's coffee** - local roaster with multiple locations across the city

## getting around.

The TTC (Toronto Transit Commission) operates the subway, streetcars, and buses. Get a PRESTO card for easy payment across all transit modes. The city is also very walkable, especially in downtown neighborhoods.
